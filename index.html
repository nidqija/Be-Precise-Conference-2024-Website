Why Be Precise?

Instead of treating all patients the same way, we look into account each patient's variations in genetic, environment & lifestyle.

Precision Medicine: One Unique Strand at a Time

Precision medicine is an emerging strategy for disease prevention and cure that considers individual
patient's unique genetics, biological and environmental factors as well as their lifestyles.
Main benefits of precision medicine includes:

Improve disease detection

Enhancing disease detection means catching illnesses early, enabling prompt treatment, reducing mortality, and cutting healthcare costs. Advanced diagnostics also aid in preventing outbreaks and contribute to a fairer healthcare system, benefiting both individuals and society.

Customize disease-prevention strategies

Customizing disease-prevention strategies involves tailoring interventions to specific populations, considering factors like demographics and local context. This targeted approach enhances the effectiveness of preventive measures, promoting better health outcomes and reducing the overall burden of diseases in a personalized and impactful way.

Prescribe more effective drugs

Prescribing more effective drugs enhances treatment outcomes by providing patients with medications that better target their specific health conditions, optimizing therapeutic benefits and improving overall healthcare efficacy.

Our Speakers

Professor Datuk Dr. A. Rahman A. Jamal is the founding director of UMBI and a professor of Paediatric Oncology and Haematology, and Molecular Biology. He has been helming the key research initiatives of UMBI since its establishment in 2003. As an esteemed researcher, clinician and one of the key drivers of molecular medicine research, he has more than 100 publications, numerous research grants and supervised more than 50 postgraduate students.

Topic : Precision Medicine Initiative for Malaysia.

Malaysia Genome and Vaccine Institute (MGVI) is a network-based not-for-profit research organisation engaging in discovery research on tropical bioresources through projects on genome sequencing, comparative genomics and structural biology.

Topic : Implementation of Malaysian genomic data on research,
development, innovation and commercialisation.

Prof. Ts. Dr Ali Bin Selamat is an academician at Malaysia Japan International Institute of Technology (MJIIT), Universiti Teknologi Malaysia (UTM), Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia. An academic institution established under the cooperation of JICA (Japanese International Cooperation Agency) and Ministry of Higher Education Malaysia (MOHE) to provide Japanese oriented engineering education in Malaysia (http://mjiit.utm.my/). Currently he is serving as the Dean for the institute.

Topic : Big Data & Artificial Intelligence (AI), overview and the future direction in healthcare system

Prof. Dr Mohd Makmor Bakry, Universiti Kebangsaan Malaysia (UKM) graduated with Bachelor Degree in Pharmacy from Universiti Kebangsaan Malaysia, Master of Clinical Pharmacy from University Science of Malaysia, and Doctor of Philosophy (Medicine & Therapeutics from University of Glasgow, Scotland, United Kingdom. Research and educational expertise include pharmaceutical care, clinical pharmacy practice, clinical pharmacokinetics, pharmacogenomics, treatment optimisation through enhancement of medication adherence.

Topic : Future direction of precision medicine pharmaceutical training

Jabatan Perlindungan Data Peribadi Malaysia (Personal Data Protection Department Malaysia or JPDP) is an agency under the Ministry of Communications and Multimedia (KKMM) with the role of ensuring compliance with the Act through increased awareness/promotion, good practice advice, and providing general advice and guidance, as well as enforcing responsibilities to oversee the processing of personal data of individuals involved in commercial transactions by data users and enforcing the Act to combat the misuse of personal data.

Topic : Privacy and confidentiality of human data in Precision Medicine: Data privacy in digitalization

Dr. Zalina Binti Mahmood the Head of Production, Plasma Fractionation & Blood Supply Management, leads the division's activities, ensuring efficient plasma fractionation and managing the blood supply chain. The Department of Radiotherapy and Oncology, Hospital Kuala Lumpur is the national referral centre in the field of oncology.
